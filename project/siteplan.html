Valle Verde Community Garden

A neighborhood resource for growing, learning, and community green-space activities.

Site Name

Valle Verde Community Garden — This name identifies a local community garden focused on sustainable growing practices and neighborhood activities. It communicates place (Valle Verde) and the activity (Community Garden).

Why chosen: the name is concise, memorable, and signals both location and purpose. It fits well for a student web project that showcases localized content, events, and membership forms.

Optional domain availability: valleverdegarden.org (check availability before purchase — not required for class)

Site Purpose

The site provides an informational hub and community portal. Primary features include:

  • Information about plots, membership, and volunteer opportunities.
  • Guides for seasonal planting and sustainable gardening practices.
  • A calendar of workshops and local events with sign-up forms.
  • A gallery of community projects and success stories.

Scope: local (neighborhood), educational, and community-organizing. The site will prioritize clear access to contact and joining information.

Scenarios (user questions)

Use-case questions from typical visitors — these guide content and navigation decisions.

  1. How do I sign up for a planting plot and what are the costs? — The site must include a clear membership/join page with a form and FAQs.
  2. When is the next community workshop on composting? — The site needs a calendar/Events page with RSVP and details.
  3. What vegetables grow best in our local microclimate? — Provide seasonal planting guides and a resources page.

Country / Place Page

Each project should include a Country/Place page (or a dedicated Local Info page) describing the region's climate, soil, and planting seasons. For Valle Verde the page will include:

  • Local climate summary (growing zone, typical temperature range)
  • Recommended crops for each season and planting calendar
  • Maps and directions to the garden (embed or static map image)
  • Local resources and partner organizations

Testing & Self-check

Evaluate and document test results on the project page. Use the following checklist:

  • HTML validation (W3C validator) — structure and syntax checks.
  • Accessibility (axe, WAVE) — headings order, alt text, keyboard navigation.
  • Color contrast — ensure text/buttons meet WCAG AA contrast ratios.
  • SEO basics — meaningful title tags, meta description, semantic headings.
  • Performance — minimize large images, enable responsive image sizes, check Lighthouse score.
  • Cross-browser testing — test on desktop and mobile (Chrome, Firefox, Safari).

At the end of the project, include short notes for each test item with links/screenshots showing fixes and results.
